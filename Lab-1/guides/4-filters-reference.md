# Four Filters: Problem Selection Framework

**Read-ahead for Lab 2. You will use this framework next Friday to evaluate and select your team's problem direction.**

Review this before Lab 2 and mentally score your own 3 brainstorm problems against these filters.

---

## How It Works

In Lab 2, your team will have 9-12 raw problem statements (3 per member). You cannot pursue all of them. The Four Filters give you a structured way to evaluate which problems are worth building a semester-long project around.

1. List all candidate problems from team brainstorming
2. Score each problem 1, 2, or 3 on each filter
3. A problem must score at least **2 on ALL four filters** to proceed
4. If multiple problems qualify, pick the one with the highest total score
5. If tied, the Program Lead makes the final call (this is in your contract)

---

## The Four Filters

### Filter 1: Real Users

*Can you name 5 specific people who have this problem?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | You are guessing. You assume people have this problem but cannot name anyone. |
| 2 | You know people in this category but have not talked to them about it specifically. |
| 3 | You can name 5 or more people AND you have heard them complain about this problem. |

**Why this matters:** If you cannot find users now, you will not find them in Week 11 when you need beta testers.

### Filter 2: AI Differentiation

*Does AI make this meaningfully better than a non-AI solution?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | A spreadsheet or basic web app could solve this equally well. AI is a "nice to have." |
| 2 | AI provides a clear advantage (speed, accuracy, scale) but a human could still do it manually. |
| 3 | This is impossible or wildly impractical without AI. The AI IS the product. |

**Why this matters:** This is an AI-powered software development course. If AI is not central, you are in the wrong course.

### Filter 3: Buildable in 15 Weeks

*Can a team of 3-4 students build an MVP of this?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | This requires custom model training, massive datasets, or infrastructure your team cannot access. |
| 2 | Challenging but realistic. Requires learning new tools but uses available APIs and free tiers. |
| 3 | Clearly achievable. Core functionality uses existing model APIs with standard web frameworks. |

**Why this matters:** An ambitious idea that ships beats a brilliant idea that stays in a slide deck.

### Filter 4: Global Ambition

*Could this work in 10 countries, not just Georgia?*

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | This is specific to one city, one institution, or one language with no path to broader relevance. |
| 2 | This has regional potential. It could work in several countries with adaptation. |
| 3 | This addresses a universal human need. With localization, it works everywhere. |

**Why this matters:** The best capstone projects demonstrate thinking at scale, even if the MVP is local.
